Flood Damage Fundamentals

Welcome to the first module in your flood adjuster training. This course covers the essential knowledge you need to begin assessing flood damage professionally.

What is Flood Damage?

Flood damage refers to the destruction caused by water entering a building from external sources — river overflow, storm surge, heavy rainfall, or rapid snowmelt. Unlike water damage from internal sources (burst pipes, appliance leaks), flood damage involves water that has contacted the ground and may carry contaminants.

The Restoration Industry

The flood restoration industry is a multi-billion dollar sector that includes:

  • Emergency services: Water extraction, board-up, temporary repairs
  • Drying & dehumidification: Industrial equipment to remove moisture
  • Demolition: Removing unsalvageable materials
  • Reconstruction: Rebuilding to pre-loss condition
  • Contents cleaning: Salvaging personal property

Key Terminology

  • NFIP: National Flood Insurance Program — FEMA's flood insurance program
  • WYO: Write Your Own — private insurers that sell NFIP policies
  • RCBAP: Residential Condominium Building Association Policy
  • ICC: Increased Cost of Compliance — coverage for elevation/floodproofing
  • SFHA: Special Flood Hazard Area — zones A and V
  • BFE: Base Flood Elevation — the computed elevation of floodwater

Types of Flood Events

  • Riverine flooding: Rivers and streams overflowing their banks — slow onset, widespread
  • Flash flooding: Rapid onset from intense rainfall — very dangerous, localized
  • Coastal flooding: Storm surge and wave action from hurricanes and nor'easters
  • Urban flooding: Overwhelmed drainage systems in developed areas

North Carolina Flood Facts

North Carolina is one of the most flood-prone states in the US. Major events include Hurricane Floyd (1999), Matthew (2016), Florence (2018), and Helene (2024). The state has over 4,000 repetitive loss properties — properties that have flooded multiple times.
